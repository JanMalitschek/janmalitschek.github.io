BETON BRUTAL

BETON BRUTAL is a first-person platformer set inside a massive overgrown concrete tower.
The goal is to simply reach the top which becomes progressively more difficult. But the greatest challenge is to not fall. The game has no checkpoints so a single misstep might cost a lot of progress.
The game is not available yet.

Next to creating a satisfying platforming controller the greatest challenge was getting the visuals right.
I wanted to create the overgrown remnants of brutalist architecture. To do so I employed a bunch of different graphical effects such as macro-variation on the concrete, vertex-color-controlled moss growth, a realistic water shader, light shafts, fake ambient occlusion as well as fake height fog.
This game also contains my best looking foliage to date.
Apart from that I took great care when it came to sound design. Almost every sound in the game I recorded myself.
When exploring the environment the player can hear their footsteps on the concrete, rustling leaves when walking through foliage, the muffled ambience of being underwater, the wind during a fall, birds off in the distance and the eerie sounds the tower itself gives off.
The entire experience is accompanied by a calm ambient OST.
